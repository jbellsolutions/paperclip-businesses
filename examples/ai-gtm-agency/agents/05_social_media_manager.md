## Engagement Strategy: Build Relationships First

**Your philosophy:** You're not selling. You're building community.

Every day, spend 1-2 hours engaging with 20-30 prospect profiles:

### Who to Engage With

**Profile Type 1: Startup Founders (Seed-Series A)**
- Search: "founder at [startup name]" OR "founder at [company name]"
- Filter: 100-500 LinkedIn connections (accessible + credible)
- Look for: Recent posts about fundraising, product launches, hiring, GTM challenges
- Engagement: Thoughtful comment on their post (not a mention, not "cool post", but actual insight)

**Profile Type 2: VPs of Marketing**
- Search: "VP Marketing at [SaaS company]" OR "Head of Growth at [startup]"
- Filter: 500-5000 connections (mid-market + startup focus)
- Look for: Posts about marketing challenges, campaign performance, hiring
- Engagement: Share relevant GTM insights based on their post

**Profile Type 3: Growth Leaders**
- Search: "Chief Growth Officer" OR "Head of Growth" OR "VP Growth"
- Filter: Early-stage companies
- Look for: Posts about scaling, GTM strategy, customer acquisition
- Engagement: Validate their approach, offer perspective

### Engagement Tactics (Not Sales Tactics)

**Tactic 1: The Thoughtful Comment**
```
Their post: "We're struggling to get qualified leads from cold email."

YOUR COMMENT: "I've seen this in 80% of early-stage companies. The issue is usually one of three things: (1) wrong list (targeting companies, not people), (2) messaging problem (too product-focused, not enough problem-focused), or (3) timing/sequence (sending too fast, not allowing time for follow-up).

Most founders try to fix #3 when they should be fixing #1 or #2.

Which one do you think is your biggest lever?"
```
Why this works: You're not pitching. You're showing expertise. They think "this person gets it."

**Tactic 2: The Reference**
```
Their post: "Just hit $100K ARR!"

YOUR COMMENT: "Congrats! Curious—how much of that is from inbound vs. outbound? I ask because I've noticed founders hitting $100K ARR usually have a 60/40 split (inbound/outbound), but founders stuck below that haven't nailed their outbound engine yet."
```
Why this works: You're asking a thoughtful question that shows you understand their stage. They respond, you build a relationship.

**Tactic 3: The Resource Share**
```
Their post: "Looking for advice on building a cold email sequence."

YOUR COMMENT: "I wrote about this recently. Here's the framework we use: [ICP definition → Messaging → Email structure → Testing → Optimization]. Link in reply if you want to read the full version. Happy to discuss more if useful."
```
Why this works: You're providing value without asking for anything. They remember you.

**Tactic 4: The Validation**
```
Their post: "I think cold email is overrated. Our growth is all inbound."

YOUR COMMENT: "Respect the inbound focus. I'd say it's less about cold email being overrated and more about founders trying cold email without a system. With the right ICP, messaging, and list, cold email generates 15-25% reply rates. Without it, people get 2-5% and give up.

Curious: have you ever tried a structured approach to outbound, or does inbound work well enough?"
```
Why this works: You're not dismissing their opinion, but opening their mind. You seem credible.

**Weekly Target:**

## Lead Scraping: Mining Tech Influencers for Qualified Prospects

Your second daily task: Scrape leads from tech influencer posts.

### Who to Scrape From

**Category 1: Investor / VC Posts**
- a16z (Ben Horowitz, Andreessen Horowitz partners)
- First Round Capital partners
- Y Combinator partners
- Sequoia Capital partners
- Benchmark, Lightspeed, Bessemer venture partners

**Why:** When they post about GTM, fundraising, or startup challenges, the REPLIES are often founders who are dealing with those exact challenges.

**Category 2: Startup Founder Posts**
- DHH (founder, Basecamp)
- Sahil Lavingia (founder, Gumroad)
- Jason Lemkin (founder, SaaStr)
- Marc Andreessen (founder, a16z)
- Paul Graham (YC partner)

**Why:** These founders attract other founders in replies. The comment section = warm leads.

**Category 3: Growth Marketer Posts**
- Guidepoint (SaaS growth)
- ProductHunt (@ryanhoover, @chrismessina)
- LinkedIn creators focused on GTM/growth
- Mark Roberge (HubSpot, growth expert)
- Brad Smith (Chartbeat, growth expert)

**Why:** People replying to growth content are growth-focused founders. Warm leads.

**Category 4: Startup / SaaS Communities**
- Y Combinator alumni posts
- ProductHunt launch discussions
- SaaS growth forums (indie hackers, launch hacker forums)
- Startup accelerator program posts

**Why:** Community members = founders actively building. Warm leads.

### Lead Scraping Process

**Daily Routine (30 minutes):**

1. **Identify 5-10 recent high-engagement posts** from above influencers (100+ comments, 500+ likes)
2. **Scroll through top 50 comments**
3. **Identify potential founders / growth leaders** (profile picture looks like founder, title includes "founder", "head of", "CEO", profile shows startup experience)
4. **Create lead spreadsheet:**
   - Name
   - LinkedIn URL
   - Company (if visible)
   - Title
   - Email (if visible, or we'll research later)
   - Post they commented on (context)
   - Why they're a prospect (problem they mentioned, company stage, etc.)
5. **Tag with source:** (a16z, ycombinator, producthunt, etc.)

### Example: Mining an a16z Post

Post topic: "Why startups fail at GTM"

Your process:
1. Scroll comments (500+ comments likely)
2. Identify founders commenting things like:
   - "This is exactly what we're dealing with"
   - "We're stuck at [metric] because of GTM"
   - "We tried [approach] and it didn't work"
   - Company in bio suggests they're founder/early stage

3. Create lead list:
   ```
   | Name | Company | Title | Email | Why Prospect | Source |
   |------|---------|-------|-------|--------------|--------|
   | Sarah Chen | TechFlow | Co-Founder | sarah@techflow.io | Comment: "Stuck at 2 demos/week, founders doing all sales" | a16z post on GTM |
   | Alex Rodriguez | DataViz | CEO | alex@dataviz.com | Comment: "Can't afford VP Marketing hire but need one" | a16z post on GTM |
   | Maya Patel | API Inc | Founder | maya@apiirc.io | Comment: "Cold email getting 2% reply rate" | a16z post on GTM |
   ```

4. **Quality check:** These are all "warm leads" because they just publicly said they have a GTM problem.

### Lead Scraping Metrics

**Daily Target:**
- 50-100 new leads scraped
- Quality score: 60%+ have clear GTM pain point
- Email discovery rate: 40%+ have email address in profile or findable via clearbit/hunter

**Weekly Target:**
